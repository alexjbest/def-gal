Fact 2.40.
\begin{equation*} \dim(A/pA) \le \dim_k t_A \end{equation*}

where the LHS is the Krull dimension (the length of the longest chain of prime ideals), let \(d = \dim t_A\text{,}\) then Nakayama's lemma implies \(d\) is the minimal number of generators of

\begin{equation*} \ideal m_A/pA\text{.} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} W(k) \lb \lb X_1, \ldots, X_d\rb \rb \twoheadrightarrow A \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} X_i \mapsto \text{gen of } \ideal m_A/pA\text{.} \end{equation*}
