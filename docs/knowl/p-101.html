Another approach

\begin{equation*} \xymatrix{ G \ar[r]^{\rho } \ar[dr]_{\bar\rho }& \GL_d(A)\ar[d]\\ & \GL_d(k) } \end{equation*}

lift of \(\bar \rho \) and say two \(\rho \)'s are the same if they are equal as maps, this is a framed deformation. We can write this more abstractly

\begin{equation*} \overline V \end{equation*}

a continuous representation of \(G\) over \(k\) with fixed basis \(\beta \text{.}\)

\begin{equation*} V \end{equation*}

a free \(A\) module with cont. action of \(G\) and a fixed isom.

\begin{equation*} V\otimes_A A/\ideal m_D \simeq \overline V \end{equation*}

and a basis \(\beta _A\) lifting \(\beta \text{.}\)
