This is not a problem as the representations we are interested in, those coming from elliptic curves and modular forms, have good reduction outside a finite set of primes \(S\text{.}\) We may encode this into our deformation problem as follows: Let \(K/\QQ\) finite and \(S\) a finite set of places of \(K\) and

\begin{equation*} K_S \end{equation*}

be the maximal extension of \(K\) unramified outside of \(S\text{.}\) Then

\begin{equation*} G_{K,S} = \Gal {K_S}K\text{.} \end{equation*}

And \(\Phi _p\) is true of \(G_{K,S}\text{.}\) The proof is an exercise in Global CFT. Silverman \(VIII\) prop. 1.6.
