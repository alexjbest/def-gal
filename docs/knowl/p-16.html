\begin{equation*} \rho \colon G_{\QQ} \to \GL_2 (\QQ_p) \end{equation*}

a continuous homomorphism. Then view \(\GL_2(\QQ_p)\) as \(\Aut(V)\) for a 2-dimensional \(\QQ_p\) vector space and fix a 2-dimensional \(\ZZ_p\)-lattice

\begin{equation*} T \subseteq V \end{equation*}

which is \(G_{\QQ}\) stable. Then we can take

\begin{equation*} \bar \rho \colon G_\QQ \to \GL_2(\FF_p) \end{equation*}

this is unique (w.r.t. the choice of \(T\)) only up to semisimplification.
