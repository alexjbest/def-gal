Now we switch to

\begin{equation*} G = G_{K,S} \end{equation*}

and we will introduce Tate's global Euler characteristic formula.

\begin{equation*} M = \text{finite cardinality} G_{K,S}\text{-module} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} S \supseteq \{\text{inf. places}\} \cup\{\ell : \ell| \# M\} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \frac{\# H^0(G, M) \# H^2(G,M)}{\# H^1(G,M)} = (\# M)^{-[K : \QQ] } \cdot \prod_{v \in S_\infty } \# H^0(G_{K_v}, M)\text{.} \end{equation*}
