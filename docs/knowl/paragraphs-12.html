Deformations.

\(A \in \mathcal C\) then

\begin{equation*} D_{\bar \rho } (A) = \left\{ (V_A, i_A) : V_A \simeq A^d \acts G,\,i_A \colon V_A \otimes k \simeq \overline V \right\}/ \sim\text{.} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} (V_A, i_A) \simeq (V_A' , i_A') \end{equation*}

when

\begin{equation*} \exists \phi \colon V_A \xrightarrow\sim V'_A \end{equation*}

as \(G\)-reps making the diagram commute.

A funny observation is that

\begin{equation*} (V_A, i_A) \simeq (V_A, i'_A)\text{.} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} (i_A')\inv \circ i_A \in \End(V_A \otimes k ) = \End(\bar \rho ) \simeq k \end{equation*}

so we are just scaling by \(c \in k^*\text{.}\) But we can take \(\phi = \lb c\rb \text{.}\)
