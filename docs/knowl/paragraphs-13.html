Larger category.

\(\mathcal C' \) the category of local profinite \(W(k)\)-algebras with residue field \(k\)

\begin{equation*} A \in \mathcal C'\implies A = \varprojlim_I A/I \end{equation*}

for \(I\) finite index. The profinite topology on \(A\) has \(\{I\}_{I\text{ finite index}}\) base of opens around 0. Morphisms are \(W(k)\)-algebra homomorphisms.

If \(A, B \in \mathcal C'\) with

\begin{equation*} f \colon A \to B \end{equation*}

a ring hom, then \(f\) is continuous.

Two topologies (profinite, \(\ideal m\)-adic). Need not be the same.

Example 2.54.
\begin{equation*} A = k \lb \lb x_1, \ldots, x_n, \ldots \rb \rb \end{equation*}

have

\begin{equation*} \ideal m_A = (x_1, \ldots) \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \ideal m_A^2 = (x_1, \ldots)^2 \end{equation*}

not finite index.
