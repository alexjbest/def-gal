Witt vectors.

\(W(k) = \) Witt vectors of \(k\) and an object of \(\mathcal C_k\text{.}\) The maximal ideal of \(W(k)\) is \(pW(k)\text{.}\)

\begin{equation*} W(k)/ pW(k) \simeq k \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} W(k)^\times \to k^\times \end{equation*}

this map has a section which is multiplicative.

\begin{equation*} [x]\mapsfrom x\text{.} \end{equation*}
Example 2.26.

If \(k \simeq \FF_{q}\text{,}\) \(q=p^n\) then

\begin{equation*} W(k) = \ints_{K_n},\, K_n = \QQ_p(\mu _{p^{n}-1}) \end{equation*}

unramified extension with

\begin{equation*} \ideal m_{W(k)} = (p) \end{equation*}

take \(x^{q-1}\) and observe that its roots are distinct mod \(p\text{.}\) For \(W(\FF_p) \simeq \ZZ_p\text{.}\)

\(\bar x\in k\text{,}\) lift to \(x\in W(k)\)

\begin{equation*} \lim_{m\to \infty } x^{q^m} = [x]\text{.} \end{equation*}
Exercise 2.27.

\(A\in \mathcal C_k\) there exists a unique ring hom

\begin{equation*} \xymatrix{ W(k) \ar[r] \ar[d] & A \ar[d]\\ k \ar[r] & A/\ideal m_A } \end{equation*}

\(A\) is a \(W(k)\)-module.

Moreover, \(A\) is a quotient of \(W(k)\lb \lb x_1, \ldots, \rb \rb \text{.}\) For \(A,A' \in \mathcal C_k\)

\begin{equation*} A \xrightarrow\phi A' \end{equation*}

in \(\mathcal C_k\) then \(\phi \) is automatically \(W(k)\)-linear.
