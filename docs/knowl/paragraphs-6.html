  1. \(D_{\bar \rho }^{\square}\) is representable (by \(R_{\bar \rho }^{\square} \))
  2. If \(\Aut_G \bar \rho = k^\times\) then \(D_{\bar\rho }\) is representable (\(R_{\bar\rho }\)).

Universal property: framed case:

\begin{equation*} \exists \rho _{univ}^{\square} \colon G \to \GL_d(R_{\bar \rho }^\square) \end{equation*}

s.t. if \(\rho _A\) is a lift of \(\bar \rho \) to \(A \)then there exists a unique

\begin{equation*} R_{\bar \rho }^\square \xrightarrow\phi A \end{equation*}

which is in \(\mathcal C\) s.t.

\begin{equation*} \xymatrix{ G \ar[r]^{\rho_{univ}^\square } \ar[dr]_{\rho _A}& \GL_d(A)\ar[d]^{\phi ^*}\\ & \GL_d(A) } \end{equation*}

unframed case: there exists a deformation \(\lb \rho _{univ}\rb \) of \(\bar \rho \) to \(R_{\bar \rho }\) s.t. if \(\lb \rho _A \rb \) is a deformation of \(\bar \rho \) to \(A\) then there exists a unique

\begin{equation*} R_{\bar \rho } \xrightarrow\phi A \end{equation*}

s.t

\begin{equation*} \phi ^*(\lb \rho _{univ}\rb) = \lb \rho _A\rb \text{.} \end{equation*}
