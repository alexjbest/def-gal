Proof
\begin{equation*} A \xrightarrow f B \supseteq \ideal m_B \end{equation*}

where the containment is finite index.

\begin{equation*} A/f\inv (\ideal m_B) \hookrightarrow B/\ideal m_B \end{equation*}

as \(k\) is finite we have \(A/f\inv (\ideal m_B)\) is finite hence artinian. So

\begin{equation*} \ideal m_A^n \subseteq f\inv (\ideal m_B)\text{.} \end{equation*}

Since finite artinian rings have nilpotent maximal ideals.

So

\begin{equation*} f(\ideal m_A^n) \subseteq \ideal m_B \end{equation*}

assume

\begin{equation*} f(\ideal m_A) \not \subseteq \ideal m_B\text{.} \end{equation*}

Take

\begin{equation*} x\in \ideal m_A \end{equation*}

s.t.

\begin{equation*} f(x)\not \in \ideal m_B \end{equation*}

hence

\begin{equation*} f(x) \end{equation*}

is a unit. So

\begin{equation*} f(x^n) \end{equation*}

is a unit, a contradiction.
